Gabe McDonnell-Maayan is a PhD candidate with the Boston University Faculty of Computing and Data Science, advised by Wesley Wildman. Gabe’s research applies complex systems methodologies, such as social simulation, network analysis, and machine learning, to a variety of fields. His work includes models of suicide, COVID-19, the opioid crisis, and political ideologies. Gabe specializes in working with subject matter experts to quantify qualitative theories into realistic and executable simulations to be used for experimentation and policy support.

Research Interests

  • Complex Systems
  • Modeling and Simulation
  • Human/Society Modeling
